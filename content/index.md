# The Future of California

California exists as an idea — shorthand for the hope that we might live a better life. From the Gold Rush to Silicon Valley, this place has been another name for opportunity. Yet today a child's prospects are too often a function of their zip code. Our government still runs on a 19th-century operating system. The dream persists; the machinery hasn't kept up.

This digital garden is an open, evolving collection of ideas for how California might close that gap — between the state we are and the state we could be. It grows from over a decade of work at the intersection of public policy, data infrastructure, and civic technology.

## The Garden

### [[california-dream|The California Dream]]
Where did the idea of California come from? Why does this state — alone among fifty — tie its identity to a dream? And what happens when that dream crashes into reality? A reflection on the myths and paradoxes that define this place.

### [[public-tech-principles|Public Technology Principles]]
From the world's largest port to pioneering aqueducts, California's history has been defined by visionary public works. The digital revolution has yet to produce their equivalent. These principles articulate what great public technology for California could look like — and strategies to build it.

### [[california-counts/index|California Counts]]
Data-driven analyses of California government spending and operations using open public data from [Open FI$Cal](https://open.fiscal.ca.gov/) and other sources. Follow the money, agency by agency.

### [[data-collaboratives|Data Collaboratives]]
Mapping public data collaborations in California and beyond. Includes the story of the California Data Collaborative — a working proof of concept for what a "data utility" might look like.

### [[reform-and-renewal|Reform and Renewal]]
Why has California's reform movement failed to tackle underlying structural challenges? An argument for pioneering the genuinely new rather than copying best practices — for getting from zero to one.

### [[education-ecosystem|The Education Ecosystem]]
The future of education is here; it's just not evenly distributed. Ideas for moving beyond the factory model toward inquiry-led, community-connected learning.

### [[seeding-second-foundation|Seeding the Second Foundation]]
An evolving series on improving government operations — building on activity in California, developments at the federal level, and experiments across our pale blue dot.

## Origin

This project began as the [California Public Technology Principles](https://argo-marketplace.github.io/future_of_california/), developed through the CA Public Technology Roundtable — a coalition of Code for America brigades, journalists, local activists, reform groups, and public officials. It is forked from [argo-marketplace/future_of_california](https://github.com/argo-marketplace/future_of_california).

The broader intellectual project draws on *[A New California Dream](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0615474663)* (2011), the *Stag Hunt: California Revolution* magazine (2013), and ongoing work at [Pioneering Spirit](https://pioneeringspirit.xyz).
